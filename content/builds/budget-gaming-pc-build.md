## Why This Budget Gaming Build Works

The Intel Core i3-12100F is one of the most efficient budget CPUs available. Despite having only 4 cores, its Hyper-Threading gives 8 threads and strong IPC from the Alder Lake architecture. It handles 1080p gaming without becoming a CPU bottleneck when paired with a mid-tier GPU.

The AMD Radeon RX 7600 delivers excellent 1080p rasterization performance and includes FSR 3 support for frame generation in supported titles. At this price point it consistently outperforms older alternatives like the GTX 1660 Super.

## Cooling Notes

The i3-12100F ships with a stock Intel cooler adequate for its 58W TDP. No aftermarket cooler is required unless you are building in a highly restricted airflow case.

## Storage Strategy

A single 1TB NVMe SSD covers the OS, a handful of game installs, and general storage. If you plan to install a large game library, consider a secondary SATA SSD down the line — both are easy upgrades on this platform.

## Platform Longevity

The LGA1700 socket supports 12th and 13th Gen Intel CPUs. A future upgrade to an i5-13600K or i7-13700K is straightforward without changing the motherboard, extending the usable life of this build significantly.
